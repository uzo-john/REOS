    CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION & CORE SYSTEM PHILOSOPHY

    1.1 Executive Vision & Product Scope

    REOS (Renewable Energy Operating System) is an integrated software platform engineered to orchestrate modern clean energy microgrids, commercial solar power stations, prosumer micro-generators, and residential/commercial energy consumers.

    Modern renewable energy grids face a fundamental challenge: bridging the physical generation of clean power with real-time IoT telemetry, automated grid dispatching, and friction-free financial settlements. REOS provides a unified solution that transforms static solar installations into dynamic, commercial-grade energy utilities.

    1.2 Target Personas & Use Cases

    PersonaRoleKey Objectives in REOS

    Energy Consumer (Receiver)Residential home or commercial business receiving powerBind smart meters, track prepaid wallet balance, request power allocations from local producers, participate in P2P energy trading.     2.2 Database Schema & Domain Entities

    REOS relies on Prisma ORM backing a PostgreSQL relational schema. Key domain models include:

  • User: Stores credentials, role (PROSUMER, CONSUMER, PRODUCER, ADMIN), contact info, and security tokens.
  • Device: Physical IoT hardware registered in the system (Type: INVERTER, SMART_METER, BATTERY, GATEWAY). Includes serial numbers, MQTT topics, and verification status.
  • SmartMeter: Specific profile linked to a Device record containing phase configuration (SINGLE_PHASE / THREE_PHASE), meter number, and installation address.
  • Plant: Producer utility station profile (Capacity kW, plant type, feeder zones, master smart meters).
  • ConsumerConnection: Junction entity mapping a Consumer's Smart Meter to a Producer Plant. Stores status (PENDING, CONNECTED, REJECTED, SUSPENDED), allocated power (kW), and request messages.
  • Wallet: Prepaid utility currency account (Currency: NGN), available balance, locked escrow balance, and billing cycle.
  • Transaction & Invoice: Audit ledger recording wallet top-ups, energy bill deductions, P2P trade settlements, and monthly statement invoices.
  • TradeOffer / BuyRequest / Escrow: P2P energy trading order book and escrow contract entities.

    3.1 Live Energy Dashboard (OverviewScreen.tsx)

    Wallet Card & Top-Up Trigger:

  • Balance Display: Live prepaid energy balance (e.g. ₦5,000.00).
  • Top Up Wallet Button: Opens the Interactive Top-Up Modal. Consumers select quick amount presets (₦1,000, ₦2,500, ₦5,000, ₦10,000, ₦25,000) or custom amounts, pick a payment gateway (Paystack, Flutterwave, Direct Bank), and receive immediate account credit with visual feedback.

    • Action Hub:

  • Register Smart Meter Button: Triggers the interactive Smart Meter Registration modal and navigates to the registration hub.
  • Request Connection Button: Opens the Producer Connection Request modal to send a dispatch invite.
  • Link Supplier Input: Enter a 6-digit Supplier Invitation Code (e.g., REOS-1234) to bind directly with a local microgrid.

    • Interactive Usage Details (KPI Cards):

  • Tapping any KPI card opens an Interactive Metric Modal:
    • - Current Load: Shows active kW draw, voltage (231V), current (5.4A), and line frequency (50.0Hz). - Daily Usage: Cumulative kWh consumed today and peak usage timeframes. - Microgrid Recv.: Clean solar energy delivered from linked suppliers at the agreed tariff (e.g. ₦180/kWh). - Grid Import: Utility backup power imported during non-solar hours. - Est. Cost Today: Daily expenditure breakdown and total financial savings versus utility grid tariffs.

    Interactive Smart Flow Diagram:

  • Tap the flow nodes (Microgrid Supplier, Smart Meter, Home Load) to inspect live MQTT telemetry streams, hardware ping latency, and power factor diagnostics.

    Step-by-Step Operating Instructions:

  • Register Physical Smart Meter:
    • - Fill in Meter Name, Serial Number (e.g., CNS-MTR-778899), Utility ID, Manufacturer, and Phase Configuration (SINGLE_PHASE / THREE_PHASE). - Click Register & Bind Smart Meter. The meter is assigned to your authenticated user account.

  • Run Connectivity Diagnostic Test:
    • - Tap Run Connectivity Verification Test on any registered meter. - The system executes a ping check against the MQTT telemetry broker and verifies latency (e.g. 12ms) and signal quality (94%).

  • Send Connection Invite to Energy Producer:
    • - Search available Producer Plants & Prosumer Microgrids by name or ID. - Click Submit Connection Request. Specify requested power allocation (e.g., 5.0 kW) and custom note.

  • Track Invite Status:
    • - View your invite under Connection Invites Sent & Live Status Tracking. - Live Status Indicators: - ⏳ PENDING: Invite sent to Producer; awaiting approval on Producer Dashboard. - ✅ CONNECTED: Producer accepted invite. Automated power dispatch and net billing active. - ❌ REJECTED: Invite declined by Producer.

    3.3 Prepaid Utility Wallet & Invoicing (BillingScreen.tsx)

  • Utility Wallet Management: Check available funds, last payment timestamp, and prepaid tariff conditions.
  • Invoice Statement Ledger: Review itemized monthly energy invoices detailing total kWh received, rate per kWh, tax breakdown, and due dates.
  • Pay Outstanding Invoice: One-click payment for unpaid or overdue invoices using utility wallet balance.
  • Transaction History: Audit trail of all prepaid purchases, trade settlements, and P2P transfers.

    • 3.4 P2P Energy Marketplace & Escrow (TradingScreen.tsx)

    REOS features a Peer-to-Peer Energy Marketplace allowing consumers and prosumers to buy and sell surplus solar energy.

  • Market Order Book: Browse open sell offers and buy requests with location tags and price per kWh (₦185 – ₦220/kWh).
  • Instant Buy & Trade: Click Buy Energy on any offer. The system calculates energy cost + platform fee (₦500) and locks funds in Escrow.
  • Escrow Settlement: Upon meter verification of energy transfer, funds are released to the seller.
  • Dispute Center: Raise a formal dispute if energy delivery fails. Administrators review telemetry logs to refund or settle escrows.

    • 3.5 AI Assistant & Energy Forecasting (AIChatScreen.tsx & AIForecastingScreen.tsx)

  • AI Energy Assistant (AIChatScreen.tsx): Powered by Google Gemini. Ask questions like: "How can I optimize my battery usage during peak hours?" or "What is my average daily expenditure?".
  • AI Forecasting (AIForecastingScreen.tsx): Predicts 7-day, 30-day, and 90-day solar generation and household load demand patterns.

    4.1 Master Producer Overview (ProducerDashboardScreen.tsx)

  • Capacity Overview: Displays installed solar capacity (kW), live generation output (kW), active connected consumers count, and daily revenue generation (₦).
  • Master Grid Control Switch: Toggle power dispatch on/off across all connected feeders.
  • Generation vs. Demand Gauge: Visual ratio of live solar production versus connected consumer load demand.

    • 4.2 Consumer Connection & Load Dispatch Hub (ProducerConsumersScreen.tsx)

    This screen represents the Producer-side interface for managing receiver connections.

    Incoming Consumer Connection Invites Tab:

  • Review pending connection invites submitted by consumers.
  • Inspect the Receiver Smart Meter Number (e.g. CNS-MTR-778899), consumer name, email, phone number, and requested kW power allocation.
  • Click [✅ Accept Invite & Connect Receiver]:
    • - The connection status updates to CONNECTED. - The consumer's receiver meter is added to the active dispatch list. - The consumer's dashboard automatically reflects the CONNECTED state.
  • Click [Decline] to reject an unsuitable connection request.

    • Connected Consumers Tab:

  • Monitor live power draw (kW) for each connected consumer receiver meter.
  • View remaining energy allocation balances (kWh).
  • Toggle individual load switches: 🔴 Disconnect Load or 🟢 Connect Load.

    • 4.3 Setup & Commissioning Wizard (ProducerSetupWizardScreen.tsx)

    A 4-step wizard for onboarding new commercial energy infrastructure:

  • Plant Details: Name, location, organization, and total capacity (kW).
  • Master Producer Smart Meter: Serial number, meter number (e.g. PRD-MTR-001), and MQTT gateway topic.
  • Inverters & PV Arrays: Onboard inverter specifications, efficiency ratings, and string configurations.
  • Battery Energy Storage System (BESS): Capacity (kWh), battery voltage (V), and maximum discharge current.

    • 4.4 Microgrid Topology & Load Balancing (ProducerMicrogridScreen.tsx)

  • Live Flow Path Diagram: Visualizes energy movement from Solar PV -> Battery Storage -> Consumer Load -> Utility Grid Net Export.
  • Busbar Phase Voltage Monitoring: Displays Phase A, B, and C AC voltages (e.g. 231.2V) to ensure grid compliance.
  • Line Loss Calculator: Calculates distribution cable heat losses and percentage loss efficiency.

    • 4.5 Engineering Tools (SolarDesignScreen.tsx)

    Includes interactive engineering calculators for installers and plant managers:

  • Solar PV Sizing Tool: Enter daily energy target (kWh) to calculate required solar panel wattage and roof footprint.
  • Battery Backup Calculator: Compute required amp-hour (Ah) battery bank size based on autonomy days and depth of discharge (DoD).
  • Cable Voltage Drop Calculator: Input current (A), cable distance (meters), voltage (V), and wire cross-sectional area (mm²) to compute voltage drop percentage.

    5.1 Protocol State Transition Matrix

    Every connection request follows a strict state machine to prevent unauthorized energy draw:

    Initial StateTrigger ActionNew StateDescription

    NONEConsumer submits invite on ConsumerMeterRegistrationScreenPENDINGConnection invite created with Receiver Smart Meter Number (CNS-MTR-XXXXXX) and requested kW allocation. PENDINGProducer clicks Accept Invite on ProducerConsumersScreenCONNECTEDProducer binds receiver meter. Automated power dispatch and net billing activated. PENDINGProducer clicks DeclineREJECTEDRequest rejected. Consumer notified on dashboard tracker. CONNECTEDProducer toggles Disconnect LoadSUSPENDED / DISCONNECTEDPower dispatch physically isolated. Telemetry continues logging standby state. SUSPENDEDProducer toggles Connect LoadCONNECTEDPower dispatch restored to receiver meter.
